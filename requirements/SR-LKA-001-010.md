# Stakeholder Requirements — Lane Keep Assist (LKA) Module

**Project:** lka-adas-sample-project
**Domain:** AD/ADAS — Lane Keep Assist
**Applicable Standards:** ISO 26262 (Functional Safety), ASPICE (SWE.1), AIAG-VDA FMEA
**Status:** Draft — for pipeline testing purposes
**Author:** Stakeholder Group (OEM Product Owner, Safety Engineer, Systems Engineer)
**Date:** 2026-07-03

---

## Assumptions & Operating Design Domain (ODD)
- Vehicle: Passenger car, SAE Level 2 ADAS
- Speed range: 60–130 km/h (highway/motorway operation)
- Road type: Structured roads with visible lane markings
- Weather: Clear to light rain; excludes heavy snow/fog (out of scope for this sample)
- ASIL rating assumed: ASIL C for core steering intervention functions (assumption for test purposes)

---

## SR-LKA-001: Lane Detection
**Category:** Functional
**Description:** The system shall detect the left and right lane boundary markings of the ego vehicle's current lane using the front-facing camera sensor, under ODD conditions defined above.
**Rationale:** Core perception capability required before any lane-keeping action can be taken.
**Source:** Systems Engineer

## SR-LKA-002: Lane Departure Detection
**Category:** Functional / Safety
**Description:** The system shall detect an unintended lane departure when the vehicle's lateral position approaches or crosses a detected lane boundary without an active turn signal.
**Rationale:** Distinguishes intentional lane changes from unintended departures.
**Source:** Safety Engineer

## SR-LKA-003: Corrective Steering Torque
**Category:** Functional
**Description:** The system shall apply corrective steering torque to guide the vehicle back toward the center of the lane when an unintended lane departure is detected.
**Rationale:** Primary actuation function of LKA.
**Source:** Product Owner
**Safety Note:** Torque magnitude shall remain within a driver-overridable limit (see SR-LKA-006).

## SR-LKA-004: Activation Speed Range
**Category:** Functional
**Description:** The system shall only be active within the vehicle speed range of 60–130 km/h.
**Rationale:** LKA is not designed for low-speed urban maneuvering or reliable at very high speeds beyond validated ODD.
**Source:** Systems Engineer

## SR-LKA-005: Driver Hands-Off Detection
**Category:** Safety
**Description:** The system shall detect driver hands-off-wheel condition within 15 seconds and issue an escalating warning sequence (visual → audible → haptic).
**Rationale:** Ensures driver remains in the control loop per SAE Level 2 requirements.
**Source:** Safety Engineer
**ASIL:** C

## SR-LKA-006: Driver Override
**Category:** Safety
**Description:** The system shall immediately suspend corrective steering torque whenever the driver applies a counter-steering input exceeding a calibrated torque threshold.
**Rationale:** Driver authority must always take precedence over automated intervention.
**Source:** Safety Engineer
**ASIL:** C

## SR-LKA-007: System Deactivation Conditions
**Category:** Functional / Safety
**Description:** The system shall automatically deactivate when lane markings cannot be reliably detected for more than 3 consecutive seconds, and shall notify the driver via HMI.
**Rationale:** Prevents unsafe operation on degraded perception input.
**Source:** Safety Engineer

## SR-LKA-008: HMI Status Indication
**Category:** Functional / Usability
**Description:** The system shall provide continuous HMI feedback indicating LKA system state (Active / Standby / Unavailable / Warning) visible to the driver at all times when the vehicle is powered on.
**Rationale:** Driver situational awareness of automation state.
**Source:** UX/HMI Stakeholder

## SR-LKA-009: Fault Detection & Degradation
**Category:** Safety
**Description:** The system shall detect internal faults (e.g., camera occlusion, sensor signal loss, actuator fault) and transition to a safe degraded state (LKA unavailable, steering returned fully to driver) within 200 ms of fault detection.
**Rationale:** Fail-safe behavior required under ISO 26262.
**Source:** Safety Engineer
**ASIL:** C

## SR-LKA-010: Data Logging for Validation
**Category:** Non-functional
**Description:** The system shall log lane detection confidence, torque intervention events, and driver override events at minimum 10 Hz for post-drive validation and field data analysis.
**Rationale:** Supports ASPICE traceability (SWE.5/SWE.6) and continuous validation.
**Source:** Validation Engineer
